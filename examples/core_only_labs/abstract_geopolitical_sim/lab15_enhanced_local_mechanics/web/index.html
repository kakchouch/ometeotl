    What this is

    An agent-based geopolitical simulation. Factions compete for territorial control over resource nodes using economic accumulation, military pressure, diplomacy, and technology investment.

    Lab 15 extends Lab 14 with three new node-level mechanics that make local conditions matter more for both conquest stability and genetic divergence.

    The Python server runs the simulation. This browser computes all display values from raw state, keeping the server a thin, concurrent-friendly state store.

    Core loop

    Each tick: nodes collect spice (spice lives in nodes, not a faction pool), factions direct nodes to ship spice toward targets and invest in R&D. Spice delivered to an enemy or neutral node becomes pressure there. When pressure exceeds the flip threshold the node changes owner; its stock is seized and deposited at the conqueror's capital.

    Lab 15 — New mechanics

    Pressure defense Spice delivered to an owned node with active pressure is consumed for defense first, at rate pressure_defense_rate (1 spice removes that many accumulated pressure points). The remainder goes to stock as normal. The AI already routes spice defensively, so defense activates automatically whenever a node is under attack.
    Pressure decay Accumulated pressure decays each tick when not continuously applied, at rate pressure_decay_rate. Attackers who stop applying spice progressively lose their toehold — sustained campaigns are required to flip a node.
    Local mutation rate Each node's effective mutation probability is scaled by a local multiplier (shown as "Mut. multiplier" in the node panel) combining four signals: high local spice concentration, devastation, incoming pressure, and number of distinct neighboring foreign factions. Contested, devastated, overstocked border nodes secede faster.
    Conflict devastation Devastation now accumulates from warfare intensity, not just ownership flips. Each unit of pressure applied (attacker) and each unit of spice spent on defense (defender) both increment the node's devastation score. A prolonged siege degrades the node even if it never changes hands; successful defense is also costly.

    Core mechanics

    Nodes Fixed positions on a graph. Each produces spice per tick; stock accumulates up to a capacity.
    Conquest Factions ship spice along multi-hop graph routes to any reachable enemy or neutral node. Delivered spice becomes pressure on that node. Pressure decays if the attacker stops and can be countered by the defender spending spice. When pressure exceeds the flip threshold the node changes owner.
    Devastation A measure of warfare intensity on a node. Increases on each ownership flip, but also accumulates from every unit of pressure applied (attacker side) and every unit of spice spent on defense (defender side). Reduces production and stock capacity; recovers slowly each stable tick.
    Genomes Each entity has a binary genome. Nodes secede if their genomes drift significantly from their faction's genome (measured by Hamming distance). Local conditions amplify mutation speed at contested or stressed nodes.
    Relations Bilateral relations [0–1] per faction pair. Relations grow passively each tick; spice pressure delivered to a faction degrades them. Affects how aggressively factions target each other.
    Technology Diplo — reduces retaliation from other factions. Cohé — increases tolerance of genetic drift. Logi — higher spice stock capacity and decreased movement costs.
    Symbolic AI Each tick a faction computes a mode from context signals (stabilize, connect, expand, reconcile, balanced) which shifts its behavior axes (E/C/L/O/Z) before planning. Current mode and goal are visible in the Symbolic AI panel.
    Globalization New edges appear stochastically over time, widening the network and opening new strategic corridors.
    Secession Nodes drift from their faction's genome through mutation. When a node's Hamming distance to the faction genome exceeds the secession threshold, it breaks off as a new independent faction with its own genome.
    Victory Last faction standing wins, or the faction with the most nodes when the tick limit is reached.
